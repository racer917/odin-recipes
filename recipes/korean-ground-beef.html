Korean Ground Beef Recipe

Introduction

This korean ground beef recipe is a game-changer for quick weeknight meals. â€”absolutely delicious, made in 30 minutes or less, and great reheated. So make a couple pounds and enjoy the leftovers the next day!

Ingredients

Remember as a general rule of thumb for any ingredients: the fresher the better. Avoid packaged food and ingredients containing seed oils.

  • 1lb. lean ground beef
  • 1/3 head of garlic minced
  • 1/4 cup of brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup of reduced-sodium soy sauce
  • 2 teaspoons sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • chili flakes
  • black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon korean chili paste

    • Optional:

  • sliced green onions
  • sesame seeds
  • sunny side up egg
  • siracha mayo
  • avocado

Steps

  1. Cook ground beef in golf-ball-sized pieces on high heat until slightly seared on the outside and still pink on the inside.
  2. While the ground beef is cooking, mince the garlic.
  3. Once, step 1 is complete, break up the ground beef and add the minced garlic, reducing the heat to medium.
  4. Separately, mix the sugar, soy sauce, ground ginger, sesame oil, chili flakes, and pepper in a bowl.
  5. Once the ground beef is a minute or two away from being done, add the sauce to the beef in the pan and mix. Then add the korean chili paste, letting it melt in the beef and sauce. Let this cook for ~ 2min until the sauce thickens a tad.
  6. Add onto a serving of rice.
  7. Optional but recommended, add green onions, sesame seeds, avocado, siracho and even a fried egg on top and enjoy!
